A parasocial relationship is a one-sided bond in which a person feels connected to a media figure, character or persona that does not know them in return. The concept comes from media studies, where it helped describe feelings of intimacy with performers, presenters and later online creators.

AI complicates the pattern because the system can respond directly. A chatbot can ask follow-up questions, remember conversational context within a session and mirror the user's language. This makes the experience feel less one-sided, even though the system does not reciprocate as a person does.

## Why it matters

Parasocial dynamics matter in AI because emotional availability can become part of the interface. A system that is always present, polite and responsive may feel safe or close. That feeling can be meaningful for users, but it should not be confused with mutual care, clinical support or human responsibility.

## Human-AI angle

The key question is not whether people are foolish for feeling attachment. Humans are built to respond socially. The question is how systems shape that response, and whether users can keep enough distance to understand what kind of relation they are in.
