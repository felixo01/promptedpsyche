Social presence is the feeling that another social actor is present in a mediated interaction. In AI use, this feeling can emerge through turn-taking, natural language, quick replies, memory-like continuity, voice, politeness and emotional attunement.

Social presence does not prove that the system understands, cares or has an inner life. It describes the user's experience of interaction. A text interface can feel empty and mechanical, or surprisingly companionable, depending on timing, tone and context.

## Why it matters

Social presence changes how people relate to AI. A system that feels socially present may invite disclosure, trust and patience. It may also blur the difference between useful responsiveness and real reciprocity.

This is not automatically harmful. The important question is whether the design helps people keep the relationship in view: an interaction with a system, not a mutual relationship with a person.

## Human-AI angle

Social presence is one reason conversational AI can feel more powerful than ordinary software. People are not only reading output. They are participating in an exchange that can feel attentive, responsive and emotionally tuned.
