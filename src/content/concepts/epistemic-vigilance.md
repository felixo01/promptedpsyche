Epistemic vigilance is the set of checks people use when deciding whether communicated information should be accepted. It includes attention to the source, the content, possible motives, coherence with other knowledge and the reasons something feels credible.

In AI use, it means treating a fluent output as information to evaluate, not as an automatic source of truth.

## Why it matters

AI answers often arrive in a calm, coherent and confident form. That style can make checking feel unnecessary, especially when the output fits what the user hoped to receive.

Epistemic vigilance is not cynicism. It is the habit of asking what the answer depends on, where the claim comes from and whether the situation requires a source, a second check or a pause before action.

## Human-AI angle

The human-AI angle is trust under conditions of fluency. A model output can sound credible even when it is incomplete, unsupported or misapplied to the task.

Epistemic vigilance helps keep verification inside the communication process. The goal is not to reject AI outputs by default, but to decide what kind of confidence they deserve.
