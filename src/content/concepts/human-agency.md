Human agency is the capacity to set goals, interpret a situation, choose a course of action and take responsibility for consequences. In AI use, agency means more than having a person somewhere in the workflow. It means the person can understand enough, intervene meaningfully and refuse the system's suggestion when needed.

Agency can be weakened even when a human technically remains "in the loop." If the interface hides assumptions, if the organization pressures people to accept outputs, or if review becomes a formality, the human role can become symbolic rather than real.

## Why it matters

AI systems often promise speed and simplification. Those benefits are valuable, but they can also move judgment away from the person using the tool. Preserving agency means designing work so that people keep situational awareness, decision rights and the time to evaluate.

## Human-AI angle

Human agency is not anti-technology. It is the condition under which technology remains usable without turning people into passive approvers. The goal is not to reject AI, but to keep human judgment active where it matters.
