Human oversight can mean different things in high-risk AI regulation and in everyday work with generative tools. In Prompted Psyche, the term is used carefully: it should mean a real ability to understand, verify and take responsibility, not just a formal human presence in a process.

## Why it matters

- It separates formal human involvement from meaningful control.
- It connects AI use with responsibility, escalation and the ability to stop a workflow.
- It helps organizations ask who can challenge AI output before it is used.
- It shows why oversight requires time, authority and enough understanding of the system.

## What not to simplify

Human oversight is not legal advice and should not be treated as evidence that an organization is ready for regulation. A person in the loop is not enough if that person cannot understand, question or stop the system's output in practice.
