Overreliance is a pattern in which a person, team or organization depends on AI output more than the system's reliability, evidence and context can justify. It is not simply using AI often. It is accepting, forwarding or acting on AI suggestions with too little checking for the situation at hand.

Overreliance appears when the system is fluent, fast, complex or difficult to inspect. People may accept the answer because it saves effort, because it looks professional, or because responsibility has become distributed across tools and workflow.

## Why it matters

AI can be useful precisely because it reduces effort. That is also why it can weaken judgment. When the system becomes the default first reader, first drafter or first decision frame, human review can turn into a ritual rather than a real evaluation.

Appropriate reliance is different. It asks when AI is good enough, when it needs human verification, and when it should not be used for the decision at all.

## Human-AI angle

Overreliance sits between trust and responsibility. A person can know that AI makes mistakes and still rely on it too much under pressure. The issue is not only belief. It is habit, workflow and accountability.
