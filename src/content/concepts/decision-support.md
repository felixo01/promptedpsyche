Decision support means using a system to inform, structure or compare options while keeping responsibility for the decision with people. In AI work, decision support may include summarizing evidence, identifying trade-offs, surfacing missing context, testing assumptions or preparing alternatives.

Decision support is different from decision substitution. A system can help organize the field of judgment, but it should not quietly become the actor that decides what matters, what risk is acceptable or who is responsible.

## Why it matters

AI is often useful before a decision is made: it can widen the view, challenge a first draft or make hidden assumptions visible. It becomes risky when the output is treated as a verdict rather than an input.

Good decision support keeps a clear boundary between recommendation and responsibility. It makes evidence inspectable, uncertainty visible and human review meaningful.

## Human-AI angle

Decision support is one of the core forms of Human-AI Interaction. It asks not only whether AI can produce an answer, but how the answer changes attention, confidence and accountability in the people who use it.
