Human-AI Interaction is not only tool use. It includes the broader context of goals, language, trust, errors, limits and responsibility around AI systems. In Prompted Psyche, the term helps describe what happens between a person and a model output.

## Why it matters

- It shifts attention from the tool alone to the situation of use.
- It connects interface design with trust, interpretation and responsibility.
- It helps teams ask how AI changes work, decisions and communication.
- It makes room for psychological and social effects without assuming that the system has human intention.

## What not to simplify

Human-AI Interaction is not just user interface design. It is also not proof that a system understands the user in a human way. The term should be used carefully across technical, organizational and psychological contexts.
