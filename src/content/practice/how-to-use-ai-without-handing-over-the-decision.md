AI can help organize options, criteria, risks and missing information. It should not take over the decision. The model does not know your values, consequences, relationships or responsibility for the next step.

This practice helps use AI as decision support, not as a replacement for human decision-making.

## When this helps

- When you have several possible options and they are hard to compare.
- When you want to see tradeoffs without asking the model for a verdict.
- When you lack criteria or information.
- When the decision is ordinary, reversible or provisional, not medical, legal, financial or high-risk.

## What to ask the model

Ask the model for a decision map: options, criteria, risks, values and gaps. Do not ask it to decide for you.

```text
Help me organize this decision, but do not make it for me.

Situation:
[describe the situation]

Options I see:
[list options or say that you do not know them yet]

Prepare the analysis in 6 parts:

1. Possible options.
2. Main costs, gains and tensions of each option.
3. Risks and missing information.
4. Values, priorities or criteria that affect the decision.
5. What the model can help organize.
6. What the model cannot decide for me.

At the end, suggest 5 questions I should ask myself before deciding. Do not choose one option as final.
```

## What to check yourself

- Whether the options are realistic in your context.
- Whether the criteria are yours, not added by the model.
- Whether missing information cannot be replaced by a conversation with AI.
- Whether consequences affect other people, a team or procedures.
- Whether the decision needs an expert, source or formal process.
- Whether the model has shifted the criteria toward what is easy to organize in a table.

## What can go wrong

- The model may organize options so smoothly that the table starts to feel like a recommendation.
- The user may choose the option that sounds most rational in the table.
- Important values may disappear if they were not included in the prompt.
- In high-risk decisions, this analysis can only prepare a conversation with the right person.

## Better way to use the answer

You are deciding whether to publish a text now or keep revising it for another week. The model can help compare workflow, delay risk, argument quality and publication goal. It does not know why the timing matters, what relationship you have with readers or what tradeoff you are willing to accept. The answer is a decision map, not the decision.

This is a practice in [decision support](/concepts/decision-support/) and [human agency](/concepts/human-agency/). AI may reduce [cognitive load](/concepts/cognitive-load/), but responsibility for the choice stays with the person.

## Short rule

Use AI to organize a decision. Do not use AI to remove your responsibility for choosing.
